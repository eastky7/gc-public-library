Ghre County Public Library secures long-term, new location

November 19, 2021 by Tracy K.

Early in the past month, board members, community leaders, and friends of the library joined together to celebrate the coming days now that an agreement has been reached with the city for the signing of a 50-year-lease.

To kick off the celebration, Piercy Stanton will be performing low-volume jazz music near the coffee maker in the breakroom. Director Conlene Muyo re-established Piercy's library rights and pardoned his ban on the grounds that he keep the coffee machine rolling and the fork out some tunes.

Piercy Stanton to perform

"...he (Piercy) better behave." -Director Conlene Muyo

Several other celebratory measures have been taken including a fairly expensive fruit display, four large banners placed throughout the inner city, and a contest that lets local children submit ideas for a chance to choose a memorial garden park theme.

Garden Park Contest Rules

Any Ghre County resident aged seventeen or younger can submit an entry. The criteria for the contest is as follows:

  • One-Page Letter Explaining Your Theme Choice
  • Some Type of Graphical Respresentation
  • Invent a Name for the Garden Park

Official City Sponsors:

  1. Community Luck Bank
  2. KRRL Gas Inc.
  3. Jim's Paintbrush Shop

Anyone eligible is encouraged to submit an entry!

Social Media Workshop Announced

November 4, 2021 by Anthony V.

Programming librarians have connected with local social media experts to give Ghre County residents a leg-up on staying connected. These workshops will reoccur weekly for the next few monthes and are accessible via registration only.

"Something about helping others stay connected just really, really makes me happy." -Margo Fennel, Super Social

Patrons who sign up will learn all about how to stay current with social media platforms such as facebook, twitter, and more. Not only will attendees learn how to stay sharp on social media, but they will also learn how to avoid the pitfalls!

Basic Computer Classes

December 15, 2021 by Bert R.

Feeling left behind with technology? Join the library's basic computer class to get started catching up with today's tech trends.

  • Learn about Laptops, Desktops, & Mobile Phones
  • Activities & Weekly Challenges
  • Laptops provided

This basic computer course aims to help patrons who have a desire to learn about technology do so inside a hands-on environment with current tech industry workers. Registration available at the circulation desk or by phone.
